Biography


I am a third-year Ph.D. student in the Statistics Department at Manchester University. I am broadly interested in statistical theory and methodology with particular interest in functional data and nonparametric statistics. Today statistics is more important that ever, because of the ease with which we can collect large amounts of data. The challenge is to sort through all of this data and separate out the consistent patterns from the noise.
How best to use these tools is a fundamental challenge for our subject and the focus of my research.
Before coming to Manchester, I did a MSc in Statistics and Probability at Center for Mathematical Research (CIMAT)
Prior to this, I did a BSc in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science from the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM).
